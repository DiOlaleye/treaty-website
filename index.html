Basic Info

In this website, you will find information on Treaty 5

What and Where is Treaty 5?

Treaty 5 is one of the numbered treaty agreements signed between the Canadian government and Indigenous peoples in the prairie provinces.

Treaty 5 covers a large area of Manitoba and parts of Ontario.

Where and When was Treaty 5 Signed?

The first negotiations for Treaty 5 took place in 1875 at Norway House, near the northern end of Lake Winnipeg. Government representatives met with leaders from several First Nations communities.

Norway House was a major trading and meeting place for Indigenous peoples and the Hudson's Bay Company. Both sides discussed land sharing and what the First Nations would receive. The treaty was later expanded between 1908 and 1910 to include more communities.

Historic Terms of the Deal

The Indigenous people involved in Treaty 5 received the following:

  • 100,000 square miles to the Crown
  • $5 per person each year
  • 160 acres for a family of five

Territory

Treaty 5 covers a large area of northern Manitoba and a small part of northwestern Ontario. It includes regions around Lake Winnipeg, Lake Manitoba, and Lake Winnipegosis. The land was important for hunting, fishing, and transportation.

Who Was Involved?

Involved parties include the following First Nations:

  • Berens River First Nation
  • Black River First Nation
  • Bloodvein First Nation
  • Cross Lake Band
  • Fisher River Cree Nation
  • Garden Hill & St. Therese Point First Nations
  • Hollow Water & Mosakahiken Creek
  • Little Grand Rapids First Nation
  • Norway House Cree Nation
